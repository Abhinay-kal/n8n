<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="NEET_UG_2026_Re-Exam_Admit_Card_Recap"><strong>NEET UG 2026 Re-Exam Admit Card: Recap</strong></h2>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">NTA released the NEET UG 2026 Re-Exam admit card on 14 June 2026 for candidates appearing in the 21 June re-exam. The admit card carried the exam centre address, reporting time, revised exam duration, and exam-day instructions. The official sources for the admit card were:</p>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><a href="https://neet.nta.nic.in" rel="nofollow noopener">https://neet.nta.nic.in</a></li>
<li><a href="https://examinationservices.nic.in/AdmitCardService/AcNeet/Login" rel="nofollow noopener">NEET Admit Card Portal</a></li>
</ul>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">If you still need to access your admit card for record-keeping purposes, these official portals remain the only authentic sources — never rely on third-party PDFs claiming to be admit cards.</p>
<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="What_Happens_After_the_NEET_UG_2026_Re-Exam"><strong>What Happens After the NEET UG 2026 Re-Exam</strong></h2>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">More than 22 lakh candidates appeared for the re-exam, conducted in a single shift from 2:00 PM to 5:15 PM across thousands of centres nationwide. With the exam over, here's the sequence candidates should track next:</p>
<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Provisional answer key:</strong> NTA is expected to publish the provisional answer key and OMR response sheets on neet.nta.nic.in, following its usual post-exam timeline.</li>
<li><strong>Objection window:</strong> Candidates will get a limited window to challenge the provisional key by paying a non-refundable fee per question challenged.</li>
<li><strong>Final answer key:</strong> NTA reviews objections and releases the final answer key.</li>
<li><strong>Result and scorecard:</strong> The NEET UG 2026 result, AIR, and scorecards follow the final answer key.</li>
<li><strong>Counselling:</strong> All India Quota counselling is conducted by the MCC, while State Quota counselling is handled by respective state authorities, including Rajasthan.</li>
</ol>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Bookmark this page or check neet.nta.nic.in regularly, as NTA typically updates these stages in quick succession once the answer key process begins.</p>
<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="How_the_NEET_Admit_Card_Download_Worked"><strong>How the NEET Admit Card Download Worked (Step-by-Step)</strong></h2>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">For reference, and for any candidate who still needs to access their admit card record, here is how the download process worked:</p>
<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li>Visit the official website <a href="https://neet.nta.nic.in/" rel="nofollow noopener">neet.nta.nic.in</a></li>
<li>Click the "Download Admit Card for NEET (UG) 2026" link under the Candidate Activity section</li>
<li>Enter Application Number, Date of Birth, and Security Pin/Password</li>
<li>Solve the captcha and click Submit</li>
<li>The admit card PDF appears on screen for download</li>
</ol>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Downloading the admit card without the application number was not possible. Candidates who misplaced theirs needed to refer to their confirmation page or registered email.</p>
<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="NEET_Admit_Card_Login_Details"><strong>NEET Admit Card Login Details</strong></h2>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Three details were required to access the admit card portal:</p>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Application Number (13-digit number from the confirmation page)</li>
<li>Date of Birth (DD/MM/YYYY format)</li>
<li>Security Pin or Password created during registration</li>
</ul>
<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="NEET_Admit_Card_Errors_Correction_Guide"><strong>NEET Admit Card Errors and Correction Guide</strong></h2>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Candidates who spotted a mismatch in name, photograph, signature, or category on their admit card were advised to contact the NTA Helpline at 011-40759000 / 011-69227700, or email neetug2026@nta.ac.in. For the most current contact details, candidates should always cross-check against the official <a href="https://neet.nta.nic.in" rel="nofollow noopener">NEET NTA website</a> rather than relying solely on numbers listed in third-party articles.</p>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Contact the NTA helpline listed on the official website</li>
<li>Avoid editing the admit card PDF directly</li>
<li>Carry valid ID proof and supporting documents to the centre if a discrepancy needed resolving on exam day</li>
</ul>
<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="Documents_Required_on_NEET_Exam_Day"><strong>Documents Required on NEET Exam Day</strong></h2>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">For candidates preparing for future NTA exams, or as a reference for the documentation NEET typically requires, the checklist included:</p>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Printed admit card with a clear photo</li>
<li>One valid photo ID (Aadhaar, Passport, PAN, School ID, etc.)</li>
<li>Passport-size photo matching the one uploaded during application</li>
<li>PwD certificate, if applicable</li>
<li>Self-declaration undertaking, if specified on the admit card</li>
</ul>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Exam-day guidelines were printed directly on the admit card — no electronic devices, no calculators, and a strict reporting time cutoff.</p>

<p class="wp-block-paragraph">NTA increased the re-exam duration to 3 hours 15 minutes, running from 2:00 PM to 5:15 PM, up from the standard duration used in the original May exam.
